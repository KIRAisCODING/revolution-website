Communication, a Bedrock for a Healthy Relationship.

    Talk about what is happening and how it's affecting you. This is very important.

  • Talk about what you want, need, and feel.
  • Accept responsibility for your own feelings.
  • Listen.
  • Share positive feelings.
  • Be aware of your tone of voice.

When we communicate, we say a lot without speaking. Non-verbal means of communicating can tell the other person how we feel about them. If our feelings don't fit our words, it is often the non-verbal that gets heard and believed. Listening is a very important part of effective communication. A good listener can encourage their partner to talk openly and honestly.

Open and clear communication can be learnt. Some find it hard to talk and may need time and encouragement to express their views. These people may be good listeners, or they may be people whose actions speak louder than their words.

Communication is a bedrock for every relationship. The way an individual responds during couple conflicts and toward the disclosure of good news of a partner in business has been linked to relationship quality. Communication is vital for healthy relationships. Being able to talk openly and honestly with the people in your life allows you to share, learn, respond, and forge lasting bonds. This is a vital part of any relationship, including those with friends and family, but it can be particularly important in romantic relationships.

Communicating better can help you achieve your goals and deepen your relationship.

In relationships, communication allows you to explain to someone else what you are experiencing and what your needs are. This act not only helps you meet your needs, but it also helps you stay connected in the relationship. Communicating clearly in a relationship means talking to each other. No matter how well you know someone, you can never read their mind. Communication needs to be very clear to avoid misunderstandings that might cause hurt, anger, resentment, or confusion.

Good communication is an important part of all relationships and is an essential part of any healthy partnership. Every relationship has ups and downs, but a healthy communication style and pattern can make it easier to deal with conflict and build a stronger partnership. It takes two people to have a relationship and each person has different communication needs and styles. Couples need to find a way of communicating that suits their relationship. Every healthy communication style requires practice and lots of hard work. Communication can never be perfect.

Always double-check that you are understanding when the other is communicating. When you talk to your partner, set aside time to talk without interruption or distraction, think about what you want to say, and be clear and true.

Questions to Ask to Improve the Way You Communicate

  • What things cause conflict in your relationship?
  • What things bring you happiness and feelings of connection?
  • What things cause you disappointment and pain?
  • How would you like your communication with your partner to be different?

Some people find some experiences and topics difficult to talk about. It may be something that is painful or makes you feel uncomfortable. Most people find it difficult to express their emotions.

If you find it difficult to communicate and talk about certain things with your partner in a relationship, you should consider talking to a counselor.
